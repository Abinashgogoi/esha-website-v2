The Mentorship Ecosystem

At ESHA Hub, we define mentorship differently. It isn't a transactional service or a fixed curriculum. It is a shared journey of discovery that happens in three distinct formats:

1. Group Guidance

Learning happens best in community. Most guidance is provided in group settings (online or offline) where questions from one member help everyone learn.

2. Project-Based Direction

You learn by doing. Guidance is often specific to the hurdles you face in your actual projects, making it practical and immediately applicable.

3. Exposure

Sometimes, mentorship is simply having the opportunity to watch experienced people work, listen to their discussions, and understand how they solve problems.
